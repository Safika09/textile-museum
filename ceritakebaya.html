Cerita Kebaya

Tarikh: 08 January 2011 - 28 February 2011

Lokasi: Galeri Saindera (Aras 1)


The Kebaya is a traditional garment worn by women in Indonesia and Malaysia,
made from sheer fabric paired with sarongs, batik,
or other traditional attire such as songket with colorful motifs.

It is believed that the kebaya originated from the Arab region,
bringing the kebaya (which in Arabic is called "abaya") to the Nusantara hundreds of years ago.
It then spread to Malacca, Java, Bali, Sumatra, and Sulawesi.
After centuries of cultural adaptation, the attire was embraced by the local communities.

Before the 1600s in Java, the kebaya was a garment exclusively worn by the royal family.
During the Dutch colonial era in Java, European women began wearing the kebaya as formal attire.
Over time, the kebaya evolved from being made of plain woven mori fabric to silk with colorful embroidery.

A similar attire called "nyonya kebaya" was first created by the Peranakan people of Malacca.
They paired it with sarongs and beautiful beaded shoes known as "kasut manek."
Today, the nyonya kebaya is undergoing a revival and is also popular among non-Asian women.

Apart from the traditional kebaya, fashion designers are exploring ways to modify its design and make it more modern.
The modified kebaya can even be paired with jeans.
