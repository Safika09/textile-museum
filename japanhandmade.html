Japan Handmade - Craft And Design From Kyoto

Tarikh: 10 May 2013 - 25 May 2013

Lokasi: Galeri Saindera


"Japan is a country with high-quality craftsmanship."

This is a quote from a book written by Muneyoshi (Soetsu) Yanagi (1889-1961),
a religious philosopher and the driving force behind Mingei (Folk Crafts) in Japan.
More than half a century has passed since Yanagi made this statement.
Although our lifestyles have drastically changed,
the spirit and techniques of Monozukuri (craftsmanship) in Japan still endure and have been passed down to this day.

The Monozukuri spirit is also applied to the production of industrial goods that require advanced technology,
and it has become the foundation of Japanese design,
characterized by perfection and a relentless pursuit of improvement.

This exhibition showcases products made using a variety of local materials
from each region—handcrafted works and traditional industrial products from Kyoto, Japan’s former capital,
where high-quality products have been continuously produced throughout its long history of tradition.

These selected products are handcrafted by passionate young artisans from Kyoto.
Their exceptional techniques and global vision have led them to collaborate with foreign experts.

How should we utilize the wisdom of heritage and culture in the present and future lives of our society?

The creative artisans featured in this exhibition have explored the possibilities of its diverse future applications,
inspiring prominent Japanese designers who have integrated tradition and technology into a broader dimension.
