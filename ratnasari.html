Ratna Sari Gallery

Ratna refers to various types of diamonds, cempaka, and precious gemstones. Sari denotes the essence or core, often referred to as the quintessence.
Ratna Sari is a fitting title for a curated collection of jewelry showcasing the creativity of artisans from the past.
The expertise of Malay, Chinese, and Indian craftsmen, along with the creativity of the Orang Asli and indigenous communities of Sabah and Sarawak,
has produced jewelry worn and passed down through generations by all ethnicities in this country.

The Ratna Sari Gallery displays jewelry and personal adornments from various ethnic groups in Malaysia,
made from gold, silver, copper, beads, and plant materials, spanning from head to toe.
These include crowns, pemeleh (traditional ornaments), hairpins, dokoh (necklaces), belts, bracelets, caps, anklets, and more.
